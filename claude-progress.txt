## Master findings (60 rounds)

### RSI Period Alignment (R54, R58)
In 1h crypto mean-reversion, aligning the oscillator lookback (e.g., StochRSI based on RSI 20) with the macro trend filters (RSI 20) significantly improves signal quality. StochMeanRev jumped from 0.47 to 0.66 Sharpe by moving from default 14-period to 20-period RSI basis.

### Dual Oscillator Complementarity (R59)
While StochRSI(20) works for a single oscillator strategy, the Hybrid (MFI + StochRSI) strategy actually performed better with a faster StochRSI(14). This suggests MFI (volume) and StochRSI (price) provide better diversification when their speeds are slightly offset.

### Exit Signal Dominance (R56, R57)
The SMA-based BB middle band remains the most robust exit signal. Switching to an EMA-based exit (EMA20) resulted in lower Sharpe, as the EMA reacts too quickly to the initial bounce, leaving profit on the table.

## Data source audit note (2026-04-23)
Code-level audit of `autoq_data/sources.py` after v0.3.0 implementation.

### Sources and findings
| Source | Granularity | API | Concern |
|---|---|---|---|
| Binance spot klines (OHLCV) | 1h | Binance Vision monthly ZIP | Monthly archive for current in-progress month does not exist yet |
| Funding rate | 8h → ffill 1h | Binance Vision monthly ZIP | Same current-month gap. **Fixed**: daily-archive fallback added in this session |
| Open interest | 1h (daily ZIP) | Binance Vision daily ZIP | Uses daily files already; works for recent dates |
| Fed net liquidity (WALCL-WTREGEN-RRPONTSYD) | Weekly → ffill 1h | FRED via pandas_datareader | Publishing lag ~4-7 days; value repeated for 168 h per week |
| US10Y / DXY | Daily → ffill 1h | yfinance (Yahoo Finance) | Market-day gaps (weekends/holidays) are ffilled; no issues |
| BTC DVOL | 1h | Deribit public REST | Optional; API can be slow; handled gracefully |
| Stablecoin market cap | Daily → ffill 1h | DefiLlama public REST | Optional; daily value repeated 24 h; 7d rolling sum used in FactorMeanRevCandidate changes at most once/day |

### Key conclusions
- **No look-ahead bias**: all ffill is forward-only (no backward fill of future data into past). The enriched sidecar represents what would have been known at each 1h bar.
- **FRED latency**: the most recent 1-2 weeks of `fed_net_liquidity` may be stale (publishing lag). Acceptable for backtesting; relevant for live deployment.
- **Current-month Binance archive gap**: monthly archives don't exist for the in-progress month. Funding rate now falls back to daily archives; OHLCV is fetched via Freqtrade's REST downloader (not Vision archive), so it is not affected.
- **Factor smoothness**: FRED weekly + DefiLlama daily factors are very coarse for 1h bars. The 7d rolling stablecoin sum in FactorMeanRevCandidate is the most useful formulation given this grain.

## Anchor concept revision (2026-04-23)
The anchor is no longer a fixed strategy — it is the **#1 ranked strategy by composite dual-window score** at the start of each session. It can change when a new strategy displaces it.

### Composite score formula (2 windows)
```
dual_score = 0.6 × main_sharpe + 0.4 × stress_sharpe
```
When validation window data is available, extend to:
```
triple_score = 0.5 × main_sharpe + 0.3 × stress_sharpe + 0.2 × val_sharpe
```

| Rank | Strategy | Main Sharpe | 2022 Sharpe | Dual Score | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | MeanRevADX | 2.1016 | -0.3836 | 1.1076 | **ANCHOR** (frozen this session) |
| 2 | FactorMeanRevCandidate | 0.9628 | 0.5867 | 0.8124 | active |
| 3 | StochMeanRev | 0.6785 | 0.6318 | 0.6598 | active |

Rules:
- The anchor's parameters are **not changed** during the current session.
- If a non-anchor strategy's dual/triple score exceeds the anchor's, it becomes the new anchor next session.
- The anchor list is updated after each session with fresh verified metrics.

### Experiment R1: Volume expansion entry filter on StochMeanRev
- **What it is**: require `volume > volume.rolling(20).mean()` at entry — the idea that a dip on high volume signals capitulation (sellers exhausted = stronger mean-reversion signal).
- **Source**: v0.2.0 retrospective finding that "volume expansion generalizes to all 3 paradigms." Hypothesis: applicable to StochMeanRev too.
- **Result**: does NOT generalize to this setup.
  - Main 2023-2025: Sharpe 0.6150 vs baseline 0.6785 (down 9.4%), trades 91 vs 95.
  - Stress 2022: Sharpe 0.5689 vs baseline 0.6318 (down 10%), trades 26 vs 27.
- **Conclusion**: not promoted. For crypto mean-reversion at this entry tightness (BB×0.997 + ADX + StochRSI), volume filtering removes valid dip-and-recover signals without improving quality. The v0.2.0 result was paradigm-specific to their looser setups.

### Experiment R2: RSI(20) > 75 exit replacing stoch_k > 0.80 on StochMeanRev
- **What it is**: use RSI(20) crossing above 75 as the overbought exit instead of StochRSI K > 0.80. v0.2.0 found RSI 75 is the BTC/ETH 1h overbought line from 3 independent directions.
- **Result**: clearly inferior, especially in the stress test.
  - Main 2023-2025: Sharpe 0.5925 vs baseline 0.6785 (down 12.7%), DD -4.05% vs -2.88% (worse).
  - Stress 2022: Sharpe 0.0963 vs baseline 0.6318 (catastrophically worse), DD -4.45% vs -1.14%.
- **Why**: RSI(20) is slower and smoother than StochRSI K. It takes longer to hit 75 after a bounce, meaning trades are held longer → more DD exposure → worse Sharpe. In 2022 bear market, holding through shallower bounces is especially harmful.
- **Conclusion**: not promoted. stoch_k > 0.80 is the correct exit for StochMeanRev.
